## MET-306
**Topic:** Meteorology - Thunderstorm Operations  
**Type:** Multiple Choice  
**Difficulty:** Basic  
**Tags:** thunderstorms, ingredients, convection

What three ingredients are required for thunderstorm development?

- A) Dry air, high pressure, and calm wind
- B) Moisture, instability, and a lifting mechanism
- C) Fog, snow, and low pressure
- D) Wind shear, ice crystals, and moonlight

**Answer:** B - Moisture, instability, and a lifting mechanism

**Explanation:** Thunderstorms need sufficient moisture, unstable air, and lift from heating, terrain, fronts, convergence, or another trigger.

**Reference:** FAA Aviation Weather Handbook, Thunderstorms; Transport Canada Flight Training Manual, Weather Considerations

- A) Cumulus stage
- B) Mature stage

## MET-344

**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Tags:** thunderstorms, emergency, inadvertent-encounter

Despite avoidance planning, you enter very heavy rain with severe turbulence near a thunderstorm. What should your priorities be?

**Answer:** Maintain aircraft control, avoid abrupt control inputs, maintain a safe attitude and appropriate speed for turbulence if specified by the aircraft manual, exit the area as safely as possible, and communicate or declare an emergency if needed.

## MET-345
**Topic:** Meteorology - Thunderstorm Operations  
**Type:** Scenario Based  
**Difficulty:** Advanced

You are planning a VFR cross-country. The GFA shows scattered thunderstorms developing along the route, radar already shows cells building west of track, the destination TAF has `TEMPO TSRA`, and daylight is limited. What is the safest decision?
