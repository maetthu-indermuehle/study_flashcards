# PPL Meteorology Flashcard Subtopics Plan

## Scope

This plan is for Canadian PPL meteorology flashcards. Canadian air law and Canadian operational weather products should take priority where they differ from FAA material. The FAA Aviation Weather Handbook is useful for weather theory and aviation hazards, but not for Canadian regulatory details.

## Proposed Subtopic List

### 1. Atmosphere, ISA, and Air Density

- Atmospheric composition and vertical structure.
- Troposphere and tropopause.
- ISA pressure and temperature values.
- Standard temperature lapse rate.
- Air density and aircraft performance.
- Density altitude, pressure altitude, true altitude, and indicated altitude.
- Effects of high, hot, and humid conditions.

### 2. Pressure Systems and Altimetry

- Pressure gradient and pressure systems.
- Highs, lows, ridges, troughs, and isobars.
- Altimeter setting changes.
- "From high to low, look out below."
- Temperature effects on true altitude.
- Rapid pressure changes near fronts and thunderstorms.

### 3. Heat, Temperature, and Stability

- Radiation, conduction, convection, and advection.
- Diurnal heating and cooling.
- Environmental, dry adiabatic, and saturated adiabatic lapse rates.
- Stable, unstable, and conditionally unstable air.
- Temperature inversions.
- Thermals and convective turbulence.
- Stability indicators pilots can observe in clouds, visibility, and ride quality.

### 4. Moisture, Humidity, and Phase Changes

- Water vapor, saturation, dew point, relative humidity.
- Temperature-dew point spread.
- Latent heat and phase changes.
- Condensation nuclei.
- Precipitation formation basics.
- Operational use of dew point spread for fog and low cloud risk.

### 5. Clouds

- Cloud formation by cooling to dew point.
- Stratiform vs cumuliform clouds.
- Low, middle, and high cloud families.
- Cumulonimbus, towering cumulus, nimbostratus, stratus, cirrus.
- Cloud bases and ceilings.
- Cloud clues for stability, turbulence, precipitation, and icing.

### 6. Fog and Low Visibility

- Radiation fog.
- Advection fog.
- Upslope fog.
- Steam fog and ice fog.
- Precipitation-induced fog.
- Mist, haze, smoke, blowing snow, dust, and volcanic ash.
- Low ceilings and mountain obscuration.
- Transport Canada white-out categories: overcast, water fog, blowing snow, precipitation.

### 7. Wind Fundamentals

- Pressure gradient force.
- Coriolis force.
- Friction effects.
- Surface wind vs winds aloft.
- Veering and backing.
- Gusts and squalls.
- Crosswind/headwind/tailwind effects on takeoff, landing, range, and diversion planning.

### 8. Local and Terrain-Induced Winds

- Sea breeze and land breeze.
- Valley breeze and mountain breeze.
- Katabatic and anabatic flow.
- Chinook/foehn-type downslope winds.
- Mechanical turbulence from terrain, buildings, trees, and rough surfaces.
- Wind indicators when no windsock or broadcast is available.

### 9. Mountain Weather

- Orographic lift.
- Mountain waves.
- Lenticular and rotor clouds.
- Lee-side turbulence and downdrafts.
- Mountain obscuration.
- Valley fog.
- Mountain icing.
- Mountain crossing decisions for light aircraft.

### 10. Air Masses and Fronts

- Continental vs maritime air masses.
- Tropical vs polar/arctic air masses.
- Cold fronts, warm fronts, stationary fronts, occluded fronts.
- Frontal lifting and cloud sequences.
- Typical weather before, during, and after frontal passage.
- Wind shifts, pressure trends, visibility trends, precipitation, icing, and turbulence near fronts.

### 11. Precipitation

- Rain, drizzle, snow, snow grains, ice pellets, freezing rain, freezing drizzle, hail.
- Showery vs continuous precipitation.
- Precipitation intensity and visibility impact.
- Freezing precipitation as an icing hazard.
- Virga and downdraft risk.

### 12. Thunderstorms and Convective Weather

- Required ingredients: moisture, instability, lifting action.
- Cumulus, mature, and dissipating stages.
- Air mass, frontal, orographic, embedded, and squall-line thunderstorms.
- Cumulonimbus identification.
- Hazards: turbulence, lightning, hail, icing, heavy rain, low visibility, gust fronts, downbursts, microbursts, tornadoes.
- Thunderstorm avoidance decisions for VFR PPL flying.

### 13. Turbulence and Wind Shear

- Convective turbulence.
- Mechanical turbulence.
- Frontal turbulence.
- Clear air turbulence.
- Wake turbulence and wind interaction.
- Low-level wind shear.
- Microburst recognition and response.
- Turbulence reporting terminology and pilot reports.

### 14. Icing and Frost

- Structural icing requirements: visible moisture and freezing aircraft surfaces.
- Supercooled water droplets.
- Rime, clear, and mixed ice.
- Freezing rain and freezing drizzle.
- Icing in stratiform clouds, cumuliform clouds, fronts, and mountains.
- Frost contamination on wings and tail.
- Carburettor icing: conditions, symptoms, prevention, carb heat use.
- Inadvertent icing encounter actions for VFR pilots.

### 15. Aviation Weather Reports and Forecasts

- METAR and SPECI decoding basics.
- TAF decoding basics.
- Pilot weather reports.
- Winds aloft.
- Radar and satellite basics.
- Surface analysis charts: fronts, pressure systems, isobars, wind barbs.
- Canadian GFA, AIRMET, SIGMET, and weather briefing products should be added from Canadian references before writing final Canadian operational questions.

### 16. PPL Weather Decision-Making

- Weather briefing before cross-country flight.
- Trend checking: current reports vs earlier reports vs forecast.
- Personal weather minimums.
- Go/no-go and divert decisions.
- VFR into IMC avoidance.
- Early 180-degree turn when weather deteriorates.
- Weather route selection and altitude selection.
- Special VFR caution.
- Weather-related emergency declarations.

## Suggested Question Mix

For each subtopic, generate a mix of:

- Basic memory questions for definitions, standard values, symbols, and cloud/fog/front names.
- Multiple-choice application questions using short pilot scenarios.
- Open-answer questions asking the student to explain cause, hazard, and pilot action.
- Practical decision questions based on METAR/TAF/GFA-style weather information.
