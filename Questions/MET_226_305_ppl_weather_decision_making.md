## MET-226
**Topic:** Meteorology - PPL Weather Decision-Making  
**Type:** Multiple Choice  
**Difficulty:** Basic  
**Tags:** personal-minima, decision-making, vfr

What are personal weather minima?

- A) Legal minima published only for IFR pilots
- B) Self-imposed limits that are usually more conservative than the legal minimums
- C) Forecast values issued by ATC
- D) Cloud heights printed on a VNC

**Answer:** B - Self-imposed limits that are usually more conservative than the legal minimums

**Explanation:** Personal minima are pilot-chosen limits for visibility, ceiling, wind, turbulence, terrain clearance, daylight, and other risk factors. They should reflect experience, currency, aircraft capability, route familiarity, and available alternates.

**Reference:** Transport Canada Flight Training Manual, Pilot Navigation - Weather; NAV CANADA Aviation Weather Services Guide, Weather Briefing Tips

Why should a VFR pilot avoid treating legal weather minima as a normal planning target?

- A) Legal minima are always wrong
- B) Legal minima leave little safety margin for workload, terrain, navigation, and changing weather
- C) Legal minima apply only to helicopters
- D) Legal minima automatically include fuel reserve

**Answer:** B - Legal minima leave little safety margin for workload, terrain, navigation, and changing weather

**Explanation:** Legal minima are minimum conditions, not comfort or training targets. A new or rusty PPL should plan with larger margins so there is time and space to notice deterioration and act early.
